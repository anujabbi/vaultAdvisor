How it earns your trust

Most people want this kind of advice — they just don't want to hand a year of statements to a startup's server. So there isn't one.

  1. See it work on sample dataHero scenarios show real analyses on a fictional persona before you share anything at all.
  2. Sign in with the AI you already trustConnect your own Claude subscription. VaultAdvisor never sees or stores an API key, and has no backend to send anything to.
  3. Upload, review, confirmDrop in a PDF or CSV. You review every field the AI read before it's saved — to a local SQLite database on your disk.
  4. Advice lights upRebalancing, tax-loss harvesting, 401(k) headroom, idle cash, withholding, Roth vs traditional — each with exact math and citations from the IRS, FINRA and investor.gov.
  5. You execute, it tracksEvery recommendation ends in a manual checklist you complete at your own broker, and check off in the app.

Advice with receipts

Every card shows three things: the why (written against your actual numbers and your stated goals and beliefs), the basis (quoted citations from reputable sources), and the execution checklist — concrete steps you can finish in an afternoon. The math is computed deterministically in code; the AI explains it, it never invents it.
